This service is an unobtrusive opportunity to inform your visitors to switch to a newer browser.

Many of your visitors are still using very old, outdated browsers, most of them for no real reason. Switching to an newer browser is better for them and for you as a webdesigner.

How it works

  1. You include the small javascript code on your website
  2. When a user visits your site with an old browser, he will be informed by a little and undisturbing bar, that his browser is not up-to-date, and that is would be better to switch. (Test the notification bar here)
  3. He can follow the advice or ignore it and it won't appear again for some days.
  4. By clicking the bar, he will get to a page with arguments why to change/update and some browser choices.

Features

  • Non-Intrusive

    The user will be notified only once, and won't be bothered any more. The notification-bar is very small and won't harm the browsing experience.

  • Informative

    Tell the user that he is using an old browser and that your website needs advanced browser-features in a "standardized way".

  • Idealistic

    Help evolve the web! Help the standards-based web! Help Open-Source software projects! Help the user! Help to increase security awareness! Support your favorite webbrowser!

  • Customize

    You can customize the browsers and versions to notifiy.
    In future releases you can also customize the text that should be shown, the frequency of the bar to appear and many other things.

  • Localized

    The message is automatically displayed in the users language.

  • Statistics

    You get statistics about what browsers your visitors are using, and about your "conversions": How many users updated or changed their browser through your advice.

Arguments, why to tell users to update

  • Reduced development costs and time

    Optimizing websites for old browsers is time consuming and thus expensive.

  • Webdesign technologies and features

    There are all this great new webdevlopment standards and they are supported by modern browsers ... but you cant use them!!
    Newer browsers let you use more features and new technologies (CSS3, SVG, HTML5, RSS, CSS Generated Content, flexible Layouts) on your website, resulting in a better browsing-experience for your users.

  • Security and benefits for the user

    Numerous benefits: security, speed, features, ... See the update page

  • The web is new and has to evolve

    That has to include the user-agents
    The web has always evolved. The strong market share of a 6 year old webbrowser (IE6) is new. If we don't do something now, we'll have to code websites like in 2001 even if it is 2012.

  • Users don't oppose updating...

    ... they often just don't know about the need to update, or the existence of the browser as a software at all.
